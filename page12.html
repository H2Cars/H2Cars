Cost of H2

H2 currently costs more than diesel or gasoline. According to [1], hydrogen prices range from $12.85 to $16 per kilogram. This translates into an operating cost of $0.13/km. Indeed, hydrogen is more expensive, as a typical car in the United States has an operating cost of $0.08/km assuming gasoline prices of $0.92/L.

As of now, H2 is expensive to make. According to a recent Shell hydrogen study, it currently costs $5-$8 per kilogram of H2 by centralized electrolysis and $1-$3 per kilogram through centralized gas reforming [2]. Again, without economies of scale, H2 prices will not drop substantially. In order to become an attractive alternative for consumers, the H2 costs must drop. It is a bit of a vicious circle, as prices are unlikely to drop until additional H2 manufacturing capacity (with scale) is created. Manufacturers, in turn, will not likely start scaling supply of H2 until demand for hydrogen cars moves toward mass production.
