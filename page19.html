CURRENT LIMITATIONS OF HYDROGEN-POWERED VEHICLES

Despite the plethora of benefits that H2-powered cars have to offer, these vehicles have some disadvantages. 

  1. PRODUCTION OF HYDROGEN - The first negative factor with H2 cars is the production of Hydrogen. Today, Hydrogen production through natural gas reforming amounts to around 95 %. This method, which uses mainly methane, contributes to greenhouse-gas emissions and global warming. Hydrogen production therefore needs to shift towards cleaner methods of producing Hydrogen (like Electrolysis). 
  2. COST - Hydrogen-powered cars today are very expensive and inaccessible to many people. (A Toyota Mirai costs ~USD 65,000$). This price needs to be brought down, perhaps through government subsidizing or less expensive vehicles (developed through research) so that these vehicles can be more widespread. You can read more about the costs involved in these vehicles by reading other articles on this website. 
  3. REFUELING INFRASTRUCTURE - Today, infrastructure to refuel H2 cars is very limited. Once again, the number of refueling stations needs to be increased so that Hydrogen-powered vehicles can gain more popularity. 
  4. TECHNOLOGY- It is obvious that Hydrogen vehicles are a new technology. Evidently, it is not anywhere near mature and needs continued development. For instance, the Fuel Cells that power these vehicles are not efficient enough and do not provide enough power yet. The entire technology surrounding Hydrogen-powered vehicles needs to be improved so that prices can be lowered and to increase the popularity of these vehicles.
