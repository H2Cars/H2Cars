HYDROGEN

THE ORIGINAL ELEMENT

Hydrogen is the most abundant element in the universe. It is also the first element, and is the source of all other elements known today. Explore its properties and characteristics below.

  • ATOMIC SYMBOL H
  • ATOMIC NUMBER 1 - Almost all Hydrogen atoms have exactly one proton and one electron. Isotopes can be found or created. Deuteurium, the first isotope, has one extra neutron, while tritium, Hydrogen's second isotope, has 2 extra neutrons. However, Protium, Hydrogen's most common form, acounts for over 99% of Hydrogen atoms
  • ATOMIC WEIGHT - Hydrogen is the lighest element known to man, with an atomic weight of ~1.007 
  • AN ABUNDANT ELEMENT - Hydrogen accounts for 74% of all elements in the universe. It is found in every living thing and is one of the elements that make up organic molecules. However, it is very uncommon in Earth's atmosphere
  • A DIATOMIC ELEMENT - Hydrogen is one of the diatomic elements, meaning that it will form a covalent bond with another Hydrogen atom to form H2.
  • REACTIVE - Hydrogen will make bonds with most atoms or molecules to form other compounds.
  • EXPLOSIVE - When mixed with Oxygen and exposed to a flame, the Hydrogen and Oxygen will react, creating Water, in an exothermic reaction. 
  • GAS- Hydrogen is a gas at room temperature. In its pure form, it is colorless and odorless. 
  • THE SOURCE OF ALL OTHER ELEMENTS - Through nuclear fusion, atoms of Hydrogen combined to make all other elements.
