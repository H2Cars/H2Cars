Hydrogen Storage Tanks

Hydrogen storage tanks are one of the most important components in Hydrogen-powered cars. Di-Hydrogen gas, the compound which they store, is a very temperamental gas that requires certain things to be contained and prevented from reacting with di-Oxygen naturally present in the atmosphere.
  • STRUCTURAL STRENGTH AND IMPACT RESISTANCE - H2-storing tanks must be strong enough to resist the violent impacts that can result from car crashes
  • PRESSURE RESISTANCE AND LEAK RESISTANCE - H2 gas is stored at about 70MPa (about 700 bars). The tanks must therefore be able to withstand this pressure without exploding or leaking
  • CAPACITY AND LIGHT WEIGHT - On top of having to be strong and pressure-resistant, H2-storing tanks must also have a large enough capacity to obtain a high enough range on the car, and must also be lightweight
