Other Components in Hydrogen-Powered Vehicles

Hydrogen cars use many components to run. We will be discussing a few of these here. You can read about other essential components like the fuel cell stacks here
  • BATTERIES- Batteries in Hydrogen cars play an important role. Most of the time, these batteries play the role of providing extra power to the motor during accelerations. In fact, the fuel cell stacks are currently unable to supply enough energy to accelerate quickly enough. In order to alleviate this problem, car manufacturers use leftover energy from the fuel cells and from decelerating to supply extra power. Today, most of these batteries are average car batteries. In the case of the Toyota Mirai, which has been discussed in a case study, the car utilizes a Nickel-Metal Hybride battery to store its electrical energy. While the capacity and lifespan of these could be improved, they are mostly satisfactory and don't require as much improvement as other components.
  • POWER CONTROL UNIT- The power control unit does most of the work in the area of electricity management. It first converts DC from the battery into AC. It also steps down voltages so that the electricity can be used by components other than the motor, like the heating system or GPS. Finally, it also controls energy outputs from the fuel cell stack and the charge/discharge of the battery, preventing it from wasting battery power.
  • ELECTRIC MOTOR- The motor used in Hydrogen cars is usually a similar motor to the ones used in electric cars. In the case of the Toyota Mirai, the motor is a synchronous AC motor used in other Toyota vehicles, which Toyota says "efficiently generates a large amount of torque from low to high speed operation". It is especially compact, and, like most electric motors, is very quiet, which contributes to another of Hydrogen-powered vehicle's.
